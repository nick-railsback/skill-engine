# Personas — how skill-engine shows up in practice

The legacy-rescue scenario (linked from the [project README](../../README.md))
is one founding-myth shape. These five personas are how the same underlying
need — *staying oriented inside a codebase too large to hold in your head* —
shows up across different professional contexts.

I've personally used skill-engine in three of these modes: daily feature work
in a vast ecosystem (like the Newly-Hired Engineer, minus the new-hire
part), efficiency hunting (like the Forward-Deployed Engineer), and the
one-time legacy rescue (the founding-myth case). The other two — the Solutions
Engineer and the Senior Product Manager — extrapolate cleanly from the same
primitives.

## The personas

### [The Senior Engineer Rescuing a Legacy Application](./legacy-rescue.md)
A 20-year-old codebase. No original builders. One-month client deadline.
Skill-engine builds the architecture document the codebase never had —
accurate enough that her teammates learn things from it.

### [The Forward-Deployed Engineer](./forward-deployed-engineer.md)
Third week in a new role. He gives the wrong answer in a customer call. His
director sees it. He uses skill-engine to reclaim credibility — and to
identify efficiency opportunities across the codebase his team is deployed in.

### [The Newly-Hired Engineer](./new-hire-engineer.md)
Newly hired engineer at an enterprise SaaS company with a 6-12 month onboarding
tradition. He treats his contextualizer as the onboarding doc the company
never wrote, and becomes a leading contributor in his first sprints.

### [The Solutions Engineer](./solutions-engineer.md)
Tasked with evaluating five competing agentic-payments protocols. Spins up
one contextualizer per protocol, composes them with his business-context
skill, and reasons about fit across the whole stack at once.

### [The Senior Product Manager](./senior-product-manager.md)
Technical-adjacent, not a coder. Composes her predecessor's strategy memos
with the live platform monorepo to surface where documented intent and
deployed reality have drifted — before championing the next quarter's
roadmap upward.
